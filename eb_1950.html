    • Cousy's College Life

    Bob Cousy went to Holy Cross college from 1946 to 1950 after earning his scholarship from his high school. At this college, he played for the Holy Cross Crusaders' varsity basketball team and was coached by Doggie Julian. His team later participated in and won the 1947 NCAA tournament, making them the first New England college to win an NCAA tournament.

    Who Was Bob Cousy?

    Robert Joseph Cousy was a professional basketball player born on August 9th, 1928. He stood at 6 feet and 1 inch tall, and mainly played the position of point guard in the league. Cousy is mostly associated with the Boston Celtics, but he also briefly played for the Cincinnati Royals. Throughout his career, he won a total of 6 championships with a point average of 18.4 points per game. His outstanding performance in the league went on to put him in the Naismith Basketball Hall of Fame in 1971.
