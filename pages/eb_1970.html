Drug Abusers in the NBA

Popular players that were known drug abusers in the NBA include Michael Ray Richardson, David Thompson, Walter Davis, Bernard King, and many more.

Tension Rising

During the 1970's, there was a lot of racism between members of the NBA as black players were slowly becoming more dominant in the league. This, as well as a rise in substance abuse caused players to become much more physically aggressive towards each other, sometimes even assualting one another which didn't put a good look on the league. With their competitors, the ABA, gaining popularity and evolving basketball in innovative new ways, and the NBA's image degrading, the NBA was backed into a corner and struggled to maintain an audience throughout the whole decade.

Substance Abuse in the League

Drug addiction in the NBA became more prevelent than it's ever been in the 1970's. Los Angeles Times estimated at least 40% to 75% of the league was addicted to some kind of drug, and 1 out of 10 players who weren't at least tried it in some way. Many players used it as a stress reliever, but some were convinced that it made them better at the game. Sense this was one of the factors for the many fights breaking out, the NBA eventually put their foot down in the late 70's by creating strict policies against drug abuse, and treating addicted players through a multitude of rehabilitation programs. These treatments and the measures they'd go through would often scale with the severity of the player's addiction and what they're addicted to. These programs proved to be quite effective, and we'd see something of a renaissance starting in the 1980's.
