Outcomes By Season

In the 1984 NBA Finals, Larry Bird and Magic Johnson would face each other for the first time on the games biggest stage. With everything each team has worked for to get here on the line, only one of them could walk away taking home the gold. The Los Angeles Lakers start off strong taking game 1 with a score of 115-109. The Boston Celtics fire back, cutting it close in game 2 with a score of 124-121. Game 3 is hard taken by the Lakers with a score of 137-104, followed up by the Celtics taking games 4 and 5 with scores of 129-125 and 121-103. The Lakers come back to even it out in game 6 with a score of 119-108, and with both teams tied in the final round, Larry Bird manages to lead his team to victory in game 7 with a score of 111-102. This defeat at the hands of Larry Bird would influcence Magic's mindset going into the next season.

In the 1985 NBA Finals, Magic Johnson would once again meet Larry Bird on the court, more motivated than last time after his last big defeat. However that motivation wasn't strong enough in the first game after the Boston Celtics took game 1 with a staggering score of 148-114. The Los Angeles Lakers quickly bounced back though taking game 2 and 3 with scores of 109-102 and 136-111. The Celtics managed to find their footing again in game 4 with a score of 107-105, but was promptly knocked back onto the ground as the Lakers finished it off with games 5 and 6 with scores of 120-111 and 111-100. With the Los Angeles Lakers winning, Magic Johnson got his revenge on Larry Bird, and would meet again in the finals 2 years later to settle the score.

In the 1987 NBA Finals, Larry Bird and Magic Johnson would meet each other one last time at the end of the season. The Los Angeles Lakers immediately asserted themselves taking game 1 and 2 with scores of 126-113 and 141-122. The Boston Celtics would try and hold their ground though taking game 3 with a score of 109-103, but the Lakers took it back in a close game, winning game 4 with a score of 107-106. The Celtics would win game 5 with a score of 123-108, but as hard as they fought, the Lakers would finish off game 6 with a score of 106-93.

Rivalry in the Making

With how bad the NBA's reputation was from all the drug abuse and violence, the league needed a miracle in order to stay in business. That miracle turned out to be Larry Bird and Magic Johnson. Long before the NBA, Bird and Johnson already had quite the rivalry throughout their college basketball years. In fact, it was quite well known too. A game between Indiana and Michigan (which each of them played for respectively) was the most watched college basketball game of all time. Their rivalry would continue in the NBA after joining around the same time. Bird on the Celtics, and Johnson on the Lakers. Viewership shifted to an all-time high, and these two saved the NBA as we know it.

Stars in the Making

Following the massive success of Larry Bird and Magic Johnson, a number of talented players began to make huge splashes in the big leagues. Players like Julius Erving became the best-known player in the league following the ABA and NBA merge. George Gervin, or "the Iceman" was greatly considered to be one of the top 75 players in NBA history. Kareem Abdul-Jabbar was regarded as one of the best players, if not the best player in the NBA, winning himself 6 NBA championships. Moses Malone was regarded as not only one of the best basketball players, but one of the best sports players of all time. Bobby Jones won an NBA championship, and was a 4 time NBA All-Star. Alex English scored more points in the 80's than any other player in the league. Fat Lever was notable for running the offense on his team, the Denver Nuggets. Dan Issel was the second-highest scorer for the Denver Nuggets and became a one-time NBA All-Star. Kevin McHale was inducted into the Basketball Hall of Fame, and was believed to be one of the greatest power forwards in NBA history. Robert Parish was tied for 2nd in most seasons played in their NBA career, only making it that far because of his strong defense game and jump shots. Danny Ainge became one of the first players to consisitently be able to shoot from the three-point line. Dennis Johnson, sometimes refered to as "DJ" would go on win 2 NBA championships as part of the Boston Celtics. Bill Walton led his team, the Portland Trail Blazers to an NBA championship, and would be inducted into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame. Clyde Drexler would be inducted twice into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame, and would later win an NBA championship in the 90's. Isiah Thomas, or "Zeke" played the role of point-guard, was a 12-time NBA All-Star, and is widely viewed as part of the top 50 greatest NBA players of all time. Joe Dumars could play a very effective shooting guard and point guard, and would later be inducted into the Basketball Hall of Fame. Bill Laimbeer became a 4-time NBA All-Star, and would win 2 NBA championships. Adrian Dantley was a 6-time NBA All-Star, a 2-time NBA scoring chmpionship, and be inducted into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame after his retirement during the late 2000's. Mark Aguirre became the 1st Mexican-American basketball player to earn an NBA title, that being a 3-time NBA All-Star, and he also won 2 NBA championships. Jack Sikma won an NBA championship, became a 7-time NBA All-Star, and was soon elected into the Naismith Basketball Hall of Fame. David Thompson, sometimes called "Skywalker" by fans is 1 of 8 players in NBA history to score over 70 points in a single NBA game, and was later inducted into the Naismith Basketball Hall of Fame. David Robinson was a 10-time NBA All-Star, 2-time NBA champion, was inducted into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame twice, and is regarded as one of the greatest centers in NBA history. Hakeem Olajuwon led the Houston Rockets to winning 2 NBA championships back-to-back, and was inducted into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame during the late 2000's. Karl Malone, sometimes called "the Mailman" would become a 14-time NBA All-Star, and was considered to be one of the greatest power forwards in the NBA of all time. John Stockton was a 10-time NBA All-Star, and was believed to be one of the best point guards of all time. Mark Eaton despite his lack of solid offense, was one of the best defensive players in the league, and soon after earned his spot as an NBA All-Star in 1989. Charles Barkley would become a 11-time NBA All-Star and be inducted into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame for his great defense and score accuracy. Michael Jordan got his start in the 80's, his 6 NBA championship wins and his phenomenal leaping ability would lead him to becoming a pillar of NBA history and a global cultural icon. Dominique Wilkins became one of the best players at slam dunking in NBA history, earning himself the title of NBA All-Star 9 times and being inducted into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame. Sidney Moncrief was known for having a great defense, and becoming a 5-time NBA All-Star. Bob Lanier would be known for playing Center for the Detroit Pistons and the Milwaukee Bucks, and was inducted later into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame. Marques Johnson would join the NBA as one of the most prominent small forwards, and would become a 5-time NBA All-Star. Ralph Sampson delivered on the heavy expectations from his college basketball career, and would become a 4-time NBA All-Star, earning his spot in the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame. Michael Cooper using his great defensive game would win himself 5 NBA championships in his time with the Los Angeles Lakers. And finally, James Worthy was a notable small forward, earning the title of NBA All-Star 7 times, and going on to win 3 NBA championships.
