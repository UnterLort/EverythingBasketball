Who Was George Mikan?

George Lawerence Mikan Jr. was a basketball superstar born on June 18th, 1924. In his career, he won 5 championships with an average score of 23.10 points per game, and played professionally for the Minneapolis Lakers. He became well known for dominating a league of shorter players and shaped much of what basketball would become today.

George Mikan Before Professional Basketball

George Mikan attended college at DePaul University. While being quite clumbsy, his coach Ray Meyer saw potential in him and transformed him into an agressive player with great offense, who also revolutionized the hookshot technique. He then lead his team to winning a championship where he would be recognized, and joined the NBA.
